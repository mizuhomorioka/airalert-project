Sound of War

Mizuho Morioka

August 10th 2023

Think about it. This sound will suddenly go off while you are at work, while you are having dinner with your family, even at 2 am in the middle of the night. Maybe nothing will happen, but maybe a missile will rain down on you.

This sound is an air raid alarm in Ukraine, which I recorded last July in Lviv. It was in midnight, and I couldn't go back to sleep until the next morning. Sadly for Ukraine people, air raid alarms are not unusual. This is the situation even in the more populated cities of Kyiv and Lviv.

When the alarm sounds, people have to flee to underground or other shelters. In Kyiv, many people flee to the subway. People have to stay there until the alarm is lifted. Some people work, study, or sleep in the evacuated basement.

But As you can see the video below, people often ignore the alerts because it is too often, and in many cases, nothing happened.

This is February 2022, when the invasion started.

You can see that Kyiv had an air raid warning for an average of nearly three hours a day.

March 2022 was even worse, when the Russian troops were closing in on Kyiv. Air raid alarms sounded for nearly a third of the day. And that would go on every day.

April was a little better than March. Still, more than two hours a day in Kyiv and one and a half hours a day in Lviv were under the warning.

My 87 year old grandmother experienced WWII as a child. When I played this sound to my grandmother she was horrified, saying it sounded just like the air raid alarms of WWII.

This time I gave data only for Kyiv and Lviv, but in the eastern and southern parts of the country, the number of air raid alarms is even higher and people live among them.
